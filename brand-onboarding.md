# Brand onboarding

Your first run takes a few minutes and ends with Lane working on your brand. Here's the path.

## 1. Pick your plan

Choose the plan that fits on the pricing page. You'll confirm it on the **plan confirmation** screen, which shows exactly what's included — active spaces, weekly Lane Cycles, and the weekly window they run on (Mon–Sun, in your timezone). You can change your mind any time before activation.

## 2. Your first Cycle

Once your plan is active, the **first Cycle** page walks you through three small steps:

1. **Confirm what Lane learned.** During signup Lane reads your website and extracts your Brand Foundation — what makes you different, your objectives, your audiences, your content pillars. Review it and refine anything that's off: a sharper foundation means a sharper Cycle.
2. **Ask your first question.** Pick one question to aim Lane's first Cycle at — for example, *"Which channel should we focus on first, and why?"* Choose a suggestion or write your own.
3. **See how the first Cycle runs.** Once your plan is active, Lane schedules a full operating pass over your brand — reading signals, drafting answers to your question, and preparing proposals. Every proposal waits for your approval before anything is published or sent. **Nothing ships without you.**

Then open your workspace — Lane takes it from there.

## 3. A tour of the workspace

Each brand gets its own workspace, organized into three groups in the sidebar:

**Brand**
- **Foundations** — what Lane knows about your brand: objectives, personas, voice, visual identity. Edit anything; Lane works from this.
- **Significant Dates** — the dates that matter to your brand (launches, events, seasonal moments) so Lane plans around them.
- **Competitors** — who Lane watches and what they've been doing lately.

**Plan**
- **Integrations** — connect your social, analytics, and advertising accounts (see [Integrations](integrations.md)).
- **Strategy** — your marketing strategy, drafted and refined with Lane.
- **Campaigns** — campaigns across your channels, from draft to published.

**Operate**
- **Intelligence** — the controls: what Lane reads, what it must ask you about, and the guardrails it works within.
- **Signals** — what Lane is noticing in your market right now.
- **Actions** — your approval queue. Every proposal Lane drafts lands here for you to approve, adjust, or dismiss.

## The approval model

Lane drafts; you decide. Posts, emails, campaigns, and outreach all wait in your approval queue until you say go. You stay the editor-in-chief — Lane is the team that never sleeps.
